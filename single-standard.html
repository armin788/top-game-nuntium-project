Xbox Game was originally supposed to be a game rental service

  • newgame

Xbox Game So could be a game rental service. But for some reason this did not happen and the service was shared with the players. GQ recently had an in-depth interview with Phil Spencer, Todd Howard and several other Xbox executives. Part of the interview talks about the days when Xbox Game was just an idea; A service that is now flourishing and is one of the cornerstones of Microsoft's gaming strategy. From this interview, we learn that Xbox has been developing gameplay since 2013, and this service was originally supposed to be similar to a service called Gamefly, which leases games. However, after the two shared services of Spotify and Netflix grew rapidly in those years, the Xbox team decided to abandon the idea of ​​launching a game rental service and instead share the same gameplay as the two platforms. Offer. Sarah Bond, head of Xbox gaming ecosystems, recalls that when she came up with the idea of ​​launching a game-sharing service with game publishers, they opposed it and reacted: "It is not possible [to work with such a service]. ! » The band says the publishers thought the game would devalue the games and make gamers no longer have to pay to buy them. Because the game had not yet been launched at the time, it was not possible to determine whether the service would have a negative impact on game sales. Of course, this debate has been going on among publishers and game developers until recently. However, the number of games added to the Xbox Gamepad on the first day of release has increased over time. Works such as Descenders and The Ascent have shown that a game can be available to game-after subscribers from day one, while also experiencing good sales.

The first gameplay was Sea of ​​Thieves, which was put to the test before its official release date. Craig Duncan, then director of Rare Studios, asked Phil Spencer if the release of Seas of Thieves would reduce the game's standalone sales, would he mind? In fact, Duncan expressed his question in an interesting way: "If every single Sea of ​​Thieves player experiences it on gameplay and we can not sell even one copy of this game, you (addressed to Spencer) have no problem?" "Definitely [I have no problem]," Spencer replies. It should be noted that Sea of ​​Thieves has become a successful and growing game since its release, and its community of players is very large.
