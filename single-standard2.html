Poor sales of Vanguard compared to Call of Duty games of the last 14 years

  • newgame

Call of Duty: Vanguard sales in the UK were lower than all Call of Duty games in the last 14 years, according to a new VGC report. Call of Duty: Vanguard was released recently and experienced lower sales at the time of release than previous games in the Call of Duty series. Newcomers to Call of Duty Vanguard were not so well received, and the average score of the PlayStation 5 Vanguard version on Metacritic (most game reviews are based on the PS5 version) is currently 75 out of 100. This is the lowest score a Call of Duty game has received since the release of Call of Duty: Ghosts in 2013. In reviewing Call of Duty: Vanguard, Zomji gave Activision a score of 7. In fact, Call of Duty Vanguard's average scores appear to be reflected in its poor commercial performance. According to a recent report on the VGC website, Call of Duty: Vanguard has experienced the weakest release among Calafudies in the UK in the last 14 years; That is, since the release of Call of Duty: Modern Warfare in 2007. What's new is that previous weak games in the series, such as Call of Duty: Ghosts and Call of Duty: Infinite Warfare, sold 52% and 16% more than Vanguard at the time of release, respectively. Games Industry website also reports that sales of Call of Duty: Vanguard in the UK fell by 40% compared to sales of Call of Duty: Black Ops Cold War during the same period. To take a closer look, compared to last year's Call of Duty, sales of physical copies of Vanguard in the UK were 26 percent lower and digital sales were 44 percent lower.

The poor commercial performance of Call of Duty Vanguard may be related to several factors; These include the increasing popularity of Battle Royale and Free-to-Play Call of Duty Warszone, the problems caused by the Corona epidemic, and of course the critical scores. However, if we do not compare Vanguard with previous Calafudio, this game has sold very well in the British market and after FIFA 22, it had the most successful release in 2021 in this country. Sales of Call of Duty versions seem to be declining year by year. One of the reasons, as mentioned, is probably due to the growing popularity of Call of Duty: Warzone. Call of Duty: Mobile can also be mentioned as another reason; A mobile game that is also available to players as Free-to-Play. Overall, Activision has shown that it still intends to invest in the free works of the Call of Duty Collection and focus on expanding them.
