Yakuza is 8 years after Like a Dragon

  • newgame

The director of Yakuza 8 announced that the story of this game will be told years after the events of Like a Dragon. An unannounced and new game is under construction at Ryu Ga Gotoku Studios. Masayoshi Yokoyama, director of Ryu Ga Gotoku Studios, announced that Yakuza 8 is a few years after the events of Yakuza: Like a Dragon. In an interview with Famitsu summarized by Ryokutya2089 and translated from Japanese to English by Gematsu Media, Yukoyama also said that the studio was developing an unannounced game; A completely new game unrelated to the Yakuza and Judgment franchises. Yakuza 8 is currently in development. The story of this game "a few years" is narrated after the events of Yakuza: Like a Dragon, and Ichiban Kasuga will once again be present in the game as the protagonist. Other than that, we do not have any specific information about Yakuza 8. We know from Yakuza: Like a Dragon that now that the series is a bit far from action gameplay. So Yakuza 8 will be a turn-based role-playing game. There are also speculations that the collection may move away from Japan to new countries. Meanwhile, Ryu Ga Gotoku has another game in the works that will apparently start a new series. However, this does not mean that the production team wants to forget the Judgment series. Yokoyama explained that gajment (spin-off yakuza) is very important to them. But they will constantly challenge themselves to create new games, as far as conditions allow.

Concerns about the future of this series of games have increased. Because Toshihiro Nagoshi, the creator of Yakuza and Daisuke Sato, the well-known producer of the Ryu Ga Gotoku team, left the studio in October. While none of them have formally announced their plans for the future, it is rumored that Nagoshi joined China's NetEase after the talks. Following these events, Ryu Ga Gotoku Studios promoted Yokuyama to the studio's executive director and producer; While Ryosuke Horii was the senior director of the Yakuza game series and Hiroyuki Sakamoto became the senior producer of the game series. Yokoyama stated that the structural change of a company is very natural. In his opinion, it was necessary to do so. Ryu Ga Gotoku Game Studio now has about 300 employees and is owned by Sega.
